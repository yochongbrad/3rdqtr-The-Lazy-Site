What's New in My New Normal?

It was one scary day, March 9, 2020, the first day of the Fourth Periodic Test. I thought that the assessment would be hard. But it was DIFFICULT. Fortunately, my memory served me right, and answered the exams. Until 3 in the afternoon when Mr. Mayor announced that classes are suspended for a week. A week had already passed, then the suspension of classes became the community quarantine that would last a month. A month had passed, the quarantine was extended another half a month. Half a month had past and the end of the enhanced community quarantine will be on further notice.

Such a grateful time, no school works. All we need to do is to eat and sleep, of course, household chores, and maintaining physical hygiene. In the first stages of quarantine, all I did was to sleep and play Pokémon to get over the tiring school days. When I got enough rest good for a year, I realize that there was no productivity. My elder brother has saved the online courses for him to watch them offline. He passed them to me so that I can effectively spend my time.

HyperText Markup Language (HTML) and Cascading StyleSheet (CSS), used to design web online, were our lessons back in the 8th grade but I did not absorb what our teacher was teaching. “Alright!” I said to myself, this is the right time to master this. Within a couple of months, more than 6 hours a day, I finished the Web Design Course by Team Treehouse. I discovered that it was not difficult, no memorization needed. All I need is to apply my logical thinking. Since my artistic side exists, the design is not an obstacle for me in the world of Web Designing. It was the birth of my own-styled responsive web, The Lazy Site. I didn’t upload it because I’m not the owner of the pictures and the typeface I used.

A whole new knowledge is what I gifted to myself. Now, I obtain such wisdom that I can use soon no matter what my job is. But before we go to the near future of expectations, we must meet the reality of the present. I was joyful to hear that we will make our website, the excitement inside me exploded until I read “WordPress,” where you can’t make your ideal logo and design using CSS unless paid. But it is a pleasant continuation in my web designing journey.
